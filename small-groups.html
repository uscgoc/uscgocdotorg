Men's Small Groups

Nathan Ho

Nathan is a proud alumnus of USC who currently works in downtown LA in civil engineering. This semester, his small group will delve into Jerry Bridges' Respectable Sins, identifying sins in our lives and repenting of them. Through weekly readings and reflections, we will find hope in the profound mercy of the gospel and the transforming grace of God as the means to overcome our sins. You can contact Nathan by sending an email to uscgoc@gmail.com.

Jeffrey West

Hey dudes, I’m Jeffrey. I’m a PhD student at USC in computational modeling of cancer progression and metastasis. My hobbies include reading & writing, biking and camping. One of the best reasons to join a small group is to have accountability of brothers who are investing in your lives and your spiritual maturity as a follower of Christ. This year, my small group will begin by reading Habits of Grace. This is a book that will guides us to come to the avenues where God pours out His grace on us believers: Scripture reading, prayer & fasting, and Christian fellowship & worship. God designed these channels, or habits, through which His love and power flow to lead us to the greatest joy of all: knowing Christ. Send me an email at uscgoc@gmail.com for more info!

Women's Small Groups

Psalm Chang

Hi there! Proverbs 4:23 says, “Watch over your heart with all diligence, for from it flow the springs of life.” That’s right, the springs of life! Because keeping our hearts free from sin is a vital part of living a God-glorifying life, we need to guard our hearts from such sins. This year, we’ll be exploring heart issues—various sins that take root in our hearts, such as pride, discontentment, and anxiety. We’ll also study the “antidotes” for the issues and aim to apply them, in order to help us live out who we are as renewed creatures. I’m looking forward to learning and growing in humility with you as we press on to pursue lives that bring most glory to our Savior. So, a little about me… I grew up in the NY-NJ area and am currently in my fourth year of a five-year Bachelor’s to Master’s degree program for Occupational Therapy. You can think of me as a senior and first-year grad student hybrid (I don’t know what to call myself either!) I enjoy making memories through taking pictures, traveling, and trying out new things. Feel free to reach out—I’d love to get to know you! :) If interested in joining, please contact me at uscgoc@gmail.com.

When: Thursdays, 7 - 9pm
Where: Main Campus (UPC) - flexible based on girls’ preferred location

Sophie Chen

Hi ladies! My name is Sophie, and I am a high school math teacher. When I’m not lesson planning or grading papers, you’ll most likely find me painting, playing guitar, or writing stories about my students. The theme for our small group this year is “The Mystery of Christ,” which we will be reading about in Paul’s letter to the Ephesians. My vision for small group is that we learn how to engage our minds in the studying God’s Word by observing, analyzing, and interpreting Scripture. My prayer is that as we seek God in His Word and meditate upon His truth, He will be faithful to change our hearts and lives. It will take a lot of hard work, but the reward of getting to know our Lord on a deeper level will make all the time and effort put in more than worth it. We will be meeting at Leavey Library on a weekday night (TBD). If you’re interested or have any questions, please feel free to email uscgoc@gmail.com to get in contact with me.

Other Ministry Opportunities

Undergraduate Connection

Hi everyone, my name is Daniel Kim. I'm currently a senior majoring in Accounting, and this year I will be heading Undergrad Connections, a weekly hangout/get-together for the undergraduate students. The purpose of this initiative is for us undergrads to spend more time with each other outside the formal Small Group, Bible Study or Sunday Worship setting and to introduce our fellowship to people who may be interested. We will have game nights, beach day, late night tacos, and number of other both organized and spontaneous events throughout the year. It will be super informal, require no weekly commitment, and be flexible to accommodate everyone's schedule. Please contact us at uscgoc@gmail.com if you are interested in getting to know and hanging out with us!

Chinese Bible Study

大家好! Kevin and Frances are humbled, joyful, and thankful to be leading Chinese Bible Study (CBS) this year!  Kevin is a PhD student who does research on drug discovery using fungi.  He enjoys contemplating and writing about truths learned from the Bible, taking walks in scenic areas, and hanging out with friends.  Frances is a registered nurse who desires to become a missionary someday, Lord-willing.  She has a huge heart for discipleship, loves spontaneous hangouts and adventures, and enjoys writing the old-fashioned way (by hand!).  We care about nothing more in life than to glorify God and see lost souls be drawn to the Gospel.  Together, our goal in CBS is to reach out to Chinese-speaking students and help them come to know Jesus Christ as their personal Savior and Lord by studying the Bible, having honest discussions, and meeting with them individually.  This semester we will be studying the Seven Miracles of Jesus through the Gospel of John.  We will learn about who Jesus is, how we know He is God, why He came into this world, and why we should believe in Him.  CBS will meet every other Wednesday on USC campus, location TBD.  If interested in joining, please contact us at uscgocCBS@gmail.com.
